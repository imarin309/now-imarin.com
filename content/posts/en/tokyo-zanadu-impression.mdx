## Preface

Last year, The Legend of Heroes: Trails in the Sky the 1st was my first time playing a Falcom game, and ever since then I've been completely drawn in by Falcom's classic RPG atmosphere set in an anime-style fantasy world.

[Read my thoughts on Trails in the Sky the 1st here (Japanese)](https://now-imarin.com/posts/sora-no-kiseki-the1st-imporession)

After finishing that, I was looking for another good fantasy-world game, and that's how I ended up playing this one, Tokyo Xanadu.

:large[To cut to the conclusion: this game was a ton of fun.]

Being a 10-year-old game, there are rough edges in the combat and graphics, but I loved the overall direction of the work, so I enjoyed it all the way through to the end.
Since it's now an 11-year-old game, I'm not writing a review here — just sharing my honest impressions!

## This Reminded Me How Much I Love This Style of Anime Game

This game is absolutely packed with that nostalgic Heisei-era light-novel-anime feel.

An ordinary high school boy meets a heroine, gains special powers, and starts fighting monsters...
It's a scenario design that's been used countless times before, but you don't see it much these days.

A beautiful transfer-student heroine, a protagonist awakening to his powers, and companions who gradually grow closer as a group.

It's packed to the brim with exactly the kind of thing otaku guys love, and it was fantastic.

## If I'm Being Greedy, I Wanted a Romance With a Heroine

This game has a system for raising your affinity with each character, heroines included, but the only payoff is a single extra event right before the final dungeon near the end.

Personally, I love every single one of the Tokyo Xanadu heroines!

- Asuka, the mysterious, textbook-perfect heroine
- Sora, serious and devoted entirely to martial arts
- Mitsuki, the student council president who seems so nurturing
- Rion, an idol who still gives off "partner-in-crime" vibes with the protagonist

Honestly, this might just be my dating-sim brain talking, but it would have been amazing if :red[you could go down an individual route with whichever heroine had the highest affinity and get a dedicated ending] for her.

Like an ending where you team up with Asuka inside Nemesis, or one where Mitsuki neatly slots herself in as the replacement for her broken-off engagement, or one that ends in a chaotic scramble after Rion's fans catch you two looking cozy together...

That's the kind of thing I was daydreaming about. ( ・∇・)

## The Refreshing Emotion of the Ending Was Great

I just went on about wanting individual routes, but :large[this game's ending was wonderfully refreshing] as it was.

The emails from your companions that play alongside the credits felt like the culmination of the whole roughly-30-hour adventure.

Seeing Yuuki's tsundere email in particular warmed my heart, and made me really glad I'd played this game.

> Kou-senpai, are you working your part-time job today?
> Things are a huge pain over here since Sis brought that guy along.
> I'm planning to deal with them halfheartedly and slip away the first chance I get.
> And seriously, who brings a cake as a gift for their own son? Doesn't that seem weird to you?

Yuuki started out as a shut-in who did nothing but hurl insults at everyone around him, but it was great watching him gradually change as he came to trust Kou and the rest of the group.

## Closing Thoughts

As for the combat, I did have a few things I wanted to say — like wishing I could use Asuka's spell-restriction release mode more often, or that protagonist Kou's weapon is a little uncool — but nitpicking a 10-year-old game felt kind of tactless, so I'll just leave a quick note about it here...
Right up to the end, I kept hoping the protagonist would awaken and switch to dual-wielding, or that his weapon would transform, but that never happened...!

I had an incredibly fun time!
I really wish more games with this kind of anime style would come out in the Reiwa era.

I'm looking forward to playing the sequel, Kyoto Xanadu: The Blooming Phantom!
