I watched Season 2 of Wistoria: Wand and Sword!

https://wistoria-anime-en.com/

This anime is packed with every classic :red[textbook fantasy trope] from the Heisei era, and it delivers the same kind of fun you'd get from a Weekly Shonen Jump manga from 20 years ago.

Season 1 aired in July 2024, so this sequel arrived after roughly two years. I hadn't watched Season 1, but with Season 2 airing now, I binge-watched Season 1 first — it was more fun than I expected, so I went straight into Season 2 as well.

## What makes this anime fun

Above all, it comes down to how satisfying it is to watch the protagonist Will in action.

In this world, your social caste is determined by whether or not you can use magic, and since Will can't use magic, he's constantly bullied and persecuted.

But even though Will can't use magic, he happens to be :red[freakishly strong in melee combat with a sword], and from the midpoint on, he goes on an unstoppable rampage.
And the students around him who used to bully him gradually change how they see him.

:large[:red[This pattern repeats throughout the show, and it feels great every time.]]

To give an example...

In Naruto, after he saves the village from Pain, there's that moving scene where Iruka-sensei declares, "Standing right in front of us is a hero!" and we readers get emotional exactly the way the author intended.

This anime runs entirely on that same kind of catharsis.

Yeah, this is exactly the kind of thing I wanted to see — that's what this 30-year-old otaku was thinking while watching. ( ・∇・)

## The early battles in Season 2 are seriously intense

Right from the start of Season 2, with barely a breather, the entire city comes under enemy attack and suffers massive damage. If you watch the anime you'll see what I mean — they get absolutely wrecked.

Then, triggered by the death of his roommate Rosti, Will awakens — and :large[:blue[how he dominates in that moment is so cool]].

His hair color changes just like a Super Saiyan, and the animation leans into a lot of those "handsome guy tilts his chin up and glares" angles, so once you're emotionally invested you can't stop grinning.

Even the delinquent-voiced enemy played by Nobuhiko Okamoto, who'd been composed the whole time, is left stunned — it was a battle packed with all sorts of satisfying moments to watch.

My only slight worry is where they go from here after power-creeping Will this hard.

## Closing thoughts

I found out after watching that this series apparently shares a world with DanMachi (Is It Wrong to Try to Pick Up Girls in a Dungeon?).

That little guy Finn, who gives off such a strong-character vibe, is apparently a DanMachi character.

Personally, I'd been avoiding DanMachi because of its title, but since Wistoria: Wand and Sword was so good, I'm now considering giving it a watch.
